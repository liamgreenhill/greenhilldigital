Progressive Web Apps

PWAs are websites which use new technology to offer an enhanced experience, generating increased revenue and engagement. We know the technical terrain and can bring these benefits to your website.

Uber, Twitter, Trivago and Pinterest are just some of the big names who in the last 18 months have evolved their websites into Progressive Web Apps and seen amazing results.

The pattern emerging from the many success stories is of PWAs achieving the following benefits:

  • increased revenue;
  • increased audience size;
  • improved performance (faster load times);
  • increased re-engagement (return visits);
  • more pages visited per session;
  • longer time-on-site;
  • increased conversion rate; and
  • decreased bounce rate.

What is a progressive web app?

Progressive Web Apps allow us to build enhanced web experiences that previously required a native application. This leads to an improved customer experience which in turn leads to increased revenue and engagement.

And since PWAs are websites, they have a larger potential audience and don’t require the platform-specific development and maintenance overhead associated with native apps. They therefore offer serious return on investment and lower cost per acquisition.

What makes PWAs different from normal websites?

PWAs are installable

A PWA can be installed to your phone or tablet’s home screen without any “app store” involvement. Browser such as Chrome can identify your website as a PWA and prompt the user to install it to their home screen. And achieving this preferential positioning on a person’s phone leads to significant increases in re-engagement, as confirmed by Twitter.

PWAs are connectivity independent

No internet connection? No problem. PWAs make use of low-level cacheing so that pages and other assets are available offline. A very app-like experience – but from a website.

Part of what we at Greenhill do is to create a cacheing strategy to find the best balance of what needs cached for the ideal offline experience, in the context of available space and other constraints. Each website has its own individual requirements.

PWAs are fast

As part of our cacheing strategy, we can radically improve how your website performs during normal connectivity, too. For pages and files which don’t change often, we can fetch them quickly from a local cache, rather than going to the network. This yields massive speed improvements which your visitors will love. And Page Load Speed is a Google ranking factor, so making your site fast not only improves the user experience but also improves your SEO.

Re-engage customers using Push Notifications

Push Notifications provide a fantastic means of bringing users back to your application. They have traditionally been the preserve of native apps, however now the functionality is available to our websites, too.

Streamlined forms and payment

We can introduce auto-login using the Credential Management API. By having our websites integrate with the user’s saved log-in details, this takes the pain out of logging in.

We can streamline payments by supporting Google Pay, Apple Pay, and touch using the Payment Request API. Removing barriers and frustration at the checkout will seriously improve conversion rate.

We already have a native app, why do we need a PWA?

You may have a native app however it’s important to note that not everyone will use that means to engage with your company. Some people will not have your native app installed or may currently be browsing on a different device.

Also: your website will likely be a person’s first experience of your company. A better web experience will likely mean more revenue and more native app installs.

Furthermore, mobile web audiences are almost three times the size of app audiences and growing twice as fast. (Source: comScore)

Being in the app store alone is no longer enough, and the cost per acquisition is higher.
